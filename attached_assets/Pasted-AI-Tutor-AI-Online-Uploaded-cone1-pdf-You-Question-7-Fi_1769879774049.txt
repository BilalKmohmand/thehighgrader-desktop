Question 7
Find the volume of the cone with a radius of 9 yd and a height of 17 yd.

Identify the Radius (r) and Height (h)
Radius = 9 yd
Height = 17 yd

Apply the Volume Formula: V = (1/3)πr²h
V = (1/3)π(9 yd)²(17 yd)
V = (1/3)π(81 yd²)(17 yd)
V = (1/3)π(1377 yd³)
V ≈ 1,443.7 yd³

Answer: 1,443.7 yd³

Question 10
A cone has a volume of 1,309 cm³. If the height of the cone is 8 cm, find its radius.

Identify the Volume (V) and Height (h)
Volume = 1,309 cm³
Height = 8 cm

Apply the Volume Formula: V = (1/3)πr²h
Solve for r²: r² = 3V / (πh)
r² = (3 × 1,309 cm³) / (π × 8 cm)
r² = 3,927 cm³ / (25.132 cm)
r² ≈ 156.2 cm²

Find r: r = √156.2 cm²
r ≈ 12.5 cm

Answer: 12.5 cm

Question 11
Katelyn must build a sandcastle pyramid with a base of 25 in × 25 in and a height of 18 in. She bought 3 bags of sand, each containing 1,200 in³ of sand. Will she have enough sand to build the castle?

Find the Volume of the Pyramid
Area of the Base (B) = 25 in × 25 in = 625 in²
Height (h) = 18 in

Apply the Volume Formula: V = (1/3) × B × h
V = (1/3)(625 in²)(18 in)
V = (1/3)(11,250 in³)
V = 3,750 in³

Total Sand Available
3 bags × 1,200 in³ = 3,600 in³

Check if Sand is Sufficient
3,600 in³ < 3,750 in³

Answer: No

Question 12
The cylinder contains two congruent hollow cones. If the height of the cylinder is 20 in and its diameter is 14 in, find the volume of the solid.

Step 1: Radius of Cylinder and Cones
Radius = Diameter/2
Radius = 14 in/2 = 7 in

Step 2: Volume of the Cylinder
Height = 20 in
Volume = πr²h
V = π(7 in)²(20 in)
V = π(49 in²)(20 in)
V ≈ 3,080.4 in³

Step 3: Volume of One Cone
Height = Half of Cylinder Height = 20 in / 2 = 10 in
Volume = (1/3)πr²h
V = (1/3)π(7 in)²(10 in)
V = (1/3)π(49 in²)(10 in)
V = (1/3)π(490 in³)
V ≈ 513.1 in³

Step 4: Volume of Two Cones
2 × 513.1 in³ = 1,026.2 in³

Step 5: Subtract Cone Volumes from Cylinder Volume
Solid Volume = Cylinder Volume - Hollow Cone Volumes
Solid Volume = 3,080.4 in³ - 1,026.2 in³
Solid Volume = 2,054.2 in³
