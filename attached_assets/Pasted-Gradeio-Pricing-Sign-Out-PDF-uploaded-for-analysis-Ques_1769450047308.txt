Question 1  
Find the volume of the pyramid with base 10 in by 11 in and height 16 in.  
Calculate the Area of the Base (B)  
The area of the rectangular base is 110 square inches.  
B = 10 in × 11 in = 110 in²  
Identify the Height (h)  
The height of the pyramid is 16 inches.  
Calculate the Volume (V)  
V = (1/3) × B × h  
V = (1/3) × 110 in² × 16 in = 1760/3 in³ ≈ 586.67 in³  
Answer: 586.67 in³

Find the volume of the pyramid with a square base 10 cm by 10 cm and height 16 cm.  
Step 1: Calculate the Area of the Base (B)  
To find the volume of a pyramid, we first need to determine the area of its base. Since the base is a square, we can use the formula for the area of a square:  
\[ 
B = \text{side} \times \text{side} 
\]  
In this case, both sides are 10 cm. Therefore:  
\[ 
B = 10 \, \text{cm} \times 10 \, \text{cm} = 100 \, \text{cm}^2 
\]  
This gives us the area of the base.
Step 2: Identify the Height (h)  
The height of the pyramid is given as 16 cm. This is the vertical distance from the base to the apex (top point) of the pyramid.
Step 3: Calculate the Volume (V)  
The formula for the volume of a pyramid is:  
\[ 
V = \frac{1}{3} \times B \times h 
\]
