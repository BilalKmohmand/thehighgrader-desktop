Question 1
Find the volume of the pyramid with a rectangular base of 10 in by 11 in and a height of 16 in.

Calculate the Area of the Base (B)
The area of the rectangular base is 110 square inches. The base is a rectangle with length 11 in and width 10 in. The area of a rectangle is calculated by multiplying its length and width. Therefore, B = 11 in * 10 in = 110 in².
Identify the Height (h)
The height of the pyramid is 16 inches. The height (h) is the perpendicular distance from the apex to the base, which is given as 16 in.
Calculate the Volume (V)
The volume of the pyramid is approximately 586.7 cubic inches. The volume of a pyramid is calculated using the formula V = (1/3) * B * h. Substituting the values, we get V = (1/3) * 110 in² * 16 in = 1760/3 in³, which is approximately 586.67 in³.
Answer
